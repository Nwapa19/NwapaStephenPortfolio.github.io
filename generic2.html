Problem Statement

The problem statement requires investigating the following key questions:

1. How does experience affect salary levels? Is there a positive correlation between the number of years of experience and salary? 2. Is there a relationship between age and salary? Do older individuals tend to earn higher salaries due to their accumulated experience or face potential age-related biases? 3. Does gender play a role in salary disparities? Are there significant differences in pay between male and female employees in similar job roles, taking into account factors such as experience and education? 4. What impact does job title have on salary? Do certain job titles command higher salaries compared to others, regardless of other factors? 5. How does education level influence salary? Is there a noticeable salary increase associated with higher levels of education, such as a bachelor's degree versus a master's degree?

By addressing these questions and conducting a comprehensive analysis of the provided salary data, we can gain a deeper understanding of how experience, age, gender, job title, and education level contribute to salary disparities. This knowledge will enable us to make informed decisions and potentially identify any areas where fair compensation practices can be improved or biases eliminated.
